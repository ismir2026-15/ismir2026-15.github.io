Blind audio effects estimation with order-aware flow matching

Authors anonymized for review
Anonymous submission
Under review

Abstract

Audio effects are crucial to music production. Typically, producers work with a personal library of effects from which they construct distinct chains to achieve a desired sound. Yet most prior approaches to automatic estimation of effect parameters are incompatible with this workflow, relying instead on fixed chains, specialised differentiable implementations, or parametric proxies. In this work, we instead approach blind audio effects estimation in a manner that reflects this practice: given a fixed library of effects, we aim to recover both the selection and ordering of effects in a chain, jointly represented in a global parameter vector. Naturally, this problem is ill-posed: the multiple possible orderings and combinations create symmetries, where many different configurations can produce the same result. We therefore adopt a generative framework based on a symmetry-aware flow matching architecture. We find that this approach outperforms regression and DDSP-based baselines, while remaining competitive with inference-time optimisation at a fraction of the inference cost and without requiring a dry reference signal. Further, we find that allowing random orderings of effects yields greater robustness to out-of-distribution audio effects approximation.

Pedalboard Audio Examples

In these examples, we conduct blind audio effects estimation on audio processed with Pedalboard effect chains.
Listening examples have been normalised peak to peak, but are otherwise unprocessed in metrics computation. AST models have been trained as regression models, using mean square error over parameters as a loss function. ST-ITO is using the wet audio as a style reference and is applied to the parameters used to modify the dry audio. Hence parameters are randomly sampled, we advise the listener to play examples at a low volume level.

DDSP Audio Examples

In these examples, we conduct blind audio effects estimation on audio processed with DDSP effect chains.
Listening examples have been normalised peak to peak, but are otherwise unprocessed in metrics computation. AST models have been trained using MRSTFT as an audio loss function.

VST Audio Examples

In these examples, we conduct blind audio effects estimation on audio processed with VST plugins. The estimated effects come from Pedalboard audio effects from which the models have been trained on.
Listening examples have been normalised peak to peak, but are otherwise unprocessed in metrics computation. All used VSTs used below are open-source. Keep in mind our model CNF was not trained on these VSTs, and had no OOD robustness incorporated in its training. We invite the listener to observe how VSTs specific effects are approximated using our Pedalboard effects library.
